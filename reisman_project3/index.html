Blindly Trusting




Vehicles have become an essential part of life. The fancy new features on today’s cars might seem evolutionary, but they are only adding to cars becoming more reliant on computers. As vehicles continue to advance in technology, they are also becoming more prone to cybersecurity threats. Once they have gained access, a hacker could control your vehicle.







A Successful Hack Could:

  • control tire pressure monitoring systems
  • disable brakes
  • manipulate vehicle diagnostics
  • change the time and song on the radio
  • change the GPS destination
  • control engine
  • control acceleration
  • unlock doors and trunk
  • access personal driving data
  • access information from phone connected via bluetooth
  • control heat and air
  • control windshield wipers






Tips and Tricks to Avoid Being Hacked

  • avoid programming home address into GPS
  • limit wireless/remote systems
  • do not leave valuable information (such as OnStar password) in the vehicle
  • use reputable car shops
  • avoid downloading untrusted apps
  • avoid car’s Web browser
  • keep up with car recalls
  • choose vehicles with Android Auto or Apple CarPlay
  • purchase an older car
